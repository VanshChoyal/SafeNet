Why cyber security matters

Cyber threats can compromise your privacy, finances, and identity. Understanding risks and practicing basic safety steps can significantly reduce harm.

Core threats

Click an item to expand and read real-life examples and prevention tips.

Phishing uses fake emails, SMS, and messaging links to trick you into revealing credentials or clicking malicious links.

  • Example: A message impersonating your bank asking to "verify account" via a link.
  • Prevention: Verify sender, hover links, enable 2FA, never share OTPs.

Malware spreads through cracked software and unknown downloads, stealing data or locking files (ransomware).

  • Example: Downloading a "free" utility that includes hidden malware.
  • Prevention: Only install from trusted sources and keep backups.

Weak or reused passwords are easily cracked or reused across stolen credentials.

  • Example: Reusing the same password on multiple sites; one breach unlocks others.
  • Prevention: Use unique strong passwords and a password manager.

Attackers manipulate trust—fake support calls, urgency tactics, or impersonation.

  • Example: A "support" call asking for remote access to fix "an issue".
  • Prevention: Verify identity and never give access on cold calls.

Unsecured Wi-Fi can let attackers intercept data or inject malicious content.

  • Example: Using a coffee shop Wi-Fi to log into banking without VPN.
  • Prevention: Use mobile data or VPN for sensitive tasks; avoid public Wi-Fi for payments.

Stay safe — actionable steps

  • Use strong & unique passwords
  • Enable 2FA
  • Don’t click unknown links
  • Verify before sharing personal info
  • Keep software updated
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for payments
  • Always check HTTPS and URLs
